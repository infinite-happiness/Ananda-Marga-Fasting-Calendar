Science of Ananda Marga Fasting

TL;DR

The fasting dates are based on the solar day (sunrise to sunrise) which occurs closest to the relevant lunar phases (ekaÌdashii, amaÌvasyaÌ and puÌrnÌimaÌ). This software uses astronomical dawn as the start of the day, and the Asia/Kolkata (Varanasi) time zone is used to determine the fasting day. In other words, the ekaÌdashii, amaÌvasyaÌ or puÌrnÌimaÌ which occurs closest to the solar day in Varanasi (based on astronomical dawn) is the selected fasting day. This software uses astronomy libraries to calculate the lunar phases used for picking the fasting dates.

Tithis

The relevant moon phases for Ananda Marga fasting are called tithis. At the time of writing, you can see on the fasting chart on the Official Website of Ananda Marga in Europe - Berlin Sector, that the start and end times are based on tithis (specifically ekaÌdashii, amaÌvasyaÌ and puÌrnÌimaÌ).

When the tithis occur, and how long they are, are not that simple to calculate. Quite some knowledge of astromony and mathematics is required (or in the case of this software, software libraries were used for the calculations. See Code.)

Calculating Without Tithis

Since it's easier to find programming libraries (such as more accurate ones) that calculate dawn, new moon and full moon, an alternative method to get the fasting date could be to calculate the geocentric phase angle at the dawns around new moon and full moon. (Which means the library has to calculate the geocentric phase angle, which the seemingly high precision library https://github.com/andrmoel/astronomy-bundle-js does. Also see https://github.com/cosinekitty/astronomy/tree/master/source/js#SearchMoonPhase This way you wouldn't calculate the time which the tithi spans over the solar day, but instead which angle the day starts and ends with. You can then calculate which solar day is the closest to the tithi by angular comparison, rather than time span.

Different Types of Dawn

Comparisons were made using sunrise and the different types of dawn. The accuracy when comparing this software's calculations with the table below were:

  • Astronomical Dawn: 88%
  • Nautical Dawn: 88%
  • Civil Dawn: 86%
  • Sunrise: 84%

Since astronomical dawn and nautical dawn had the same accuracy, astronomical dawn was the selected method to use. The rationale is that it makes the most sense if the beginning of the day is the actual beginning of something, and the astronomical dawn is the first dawn.

Differences

The reasons 100% accuracy wasn't achieved:

  • The calculations of tithis and sunrise are not exactly the same. Usually the difference is small, and still the chosen fasting day is different because the tithi spans over similarly over both two solar days. The sunrise also depends on altitude, which is not taken into account in this software.
  • Some of the official fasting days are miscalculated (probably mistaken because of human error).
